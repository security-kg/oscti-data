The simplest to use pencil and paper crypto is the One Time Pad. You simply write your message under the Key Text and do the required “modular addition” of the two, either in your head or by “counting” along an alphabet strip or using a “Ceaser Square”.

As long as the keystream is sufficiently random[1] and only used once, the system is considered secure because all messages of the same length are equiprobable.

The problem with such a system is that you need a large amount of Key Material (KeyMat) which needs to be kept secure. Which can be a logistics nightmare if you do not do Key Managment (KeyMan) correctly.

Thus you might need to use a more complicated system that you can carry around in your head. If you do you need to make four basic steps.

1, Flatten the Plain Text statistics.
2, Generate the Key Stream.
3, Make the Cipher Text.
4, Change the statistics of the Cipher Text.

I won’t go through all of it again as I have in the past on this blog. But the important thing to remember is that you should keep messages short such that idealy they fit in an SMS or tweet. As anything longer just makes the odds of the determanistic key stream generation being broken.

@ Rachel,

I was keen to learn more about Clives taken on situational awareness

It’s a little like asking @ Bruce about “thinking hinky”.

Most humans come with what you might call three brains, only one of which we think we have any control over. It’s what you might call your conscious mind, you use it for learning in a knowledge not a lower skill level, you also use it for thinking and reasoning, and gives you that voice in your head. Some people think in words, some in pictures and a very few can “see” mathmatics.

Siting below your conscious mind is a mess that you might call your monkey brain, it’s the one that sends you scampering up a tree before your conscious mind even sees the stripes on the tiger in the bush. It’s the one that runs semi autonomously and is realy responsible for your survival. In many ways it’s pre-programed with over reaction which is why many many things that are not harmful make your body behave like it has been poisoned, the classic example being intertia sickness. The signals from the lump of chalk in your inner ear do not tie up with what your eyes and skin perceive, the monkey brain assumes that the difference is due to something you injested, hence you honk. For the same reason alcohol causes the same problem, hence your body honks to empty it’s self of “fermenting food” which is likely to contain other toxins than just alcohol. It’s also the brain that gives you fine motor skills, so it can be taught, but the process is one of moving from the conscious mind to the monkey brain. By and large the training of the monkey brain is about repetition, it’s how we become adept at riding a bike, playing a musical instrument, or even catching a ball. It’s also why armedforces “Drill” the apparent mindlessness of it is how you get a skill into the monkey brain, to the point it takes over the skill in a way that frees up the conscious mind.

Problems arise when your monkey brain detects something that fpr some reason does not fit or feels wrong. It’s the hinky feeling that makes you want to put your hand on the back of your neck. It’s this that you need to pick up on and train to get good situational awareness.

Which brings us to the third brain, we don’t realy use it for thinking, it’s primary job is to control the chemical factory that is our digestive system. The main methods of signalling are via chemicals either tucked in under the brain or in the likes of the liver and heart etc. However there are nerve connections, that occasionaly cause a real effect from both the conscious and monkey brains, it quite literally gives us the “gut feeling”.

Thus situational awareness is getting your bodies semi-autonomous “brains” to learn when things are hinky / not right, and flash a warning into your conscious mind such that you can deal with thinks rationaly whilst you still have time to be proactive not reactive.

Which just leaves the “learn / train” aspect, which would fill a book shelf with things. But the important lesson is repitition to the point of mindlessness –of the conscious mind– is good. It’s one of the reasons they talk about “10,000 hours” to become little more than a journeyman in any skill. However once you have got past the journyman stage, you have to change the learning style. That is no longer “drill” bit to periodic reinforcment seperated by other activities, and never to the point where it becomes mentaly fatigueing.
