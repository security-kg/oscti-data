NotPosix ‚Ä¢ May 29, 2008 2:33 AM

@anon: “Doesn’t Windows support POSIX to some degree?”

This was misleading marketing statements from Microsoft.

sleep is POSIX.2, and Microsoft said that they would not comply to POSIX.2 because it is not an ISO standard: http://www.microsoft.com/technet/archive/ntwrkstn/reskit/poscomp.mspx
The same page explains why Microsoft would not comply to POSIX.N for N=2, 3, …, 12.

In NT, only the kernel is POSIX.1 (basically, it is C calls).

XP and windows 2003 are no more POSIX according to http://support.microsoft.com/kb/308259 and http://www.google.fr/search?hl=fr&q=%22POSIX+and+OS%2F2+are+not+supported+in+Windows+XP+or+in+Windows+Server+2003%22 unless Windows Services for Unix (SFU, or its successor SUA) is downloaded.

Their new tool Powershell is not POSIX either, despite having a misleading name.

You can still have a look at some marketing misleading statements here : http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/bb463202.aspx
